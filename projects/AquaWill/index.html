Aqua's Will

A Unity 2D Platform-adventure Game

Aqua's Will is a student program at SHU. By creating a single-player game, our goal is to make people realize the importance of protecting the environment with a compelling backstory and detailed action.

This was a 6 person team project where we created an ocean pollution-related game for the 2023 Tencent Youth Game Design Challenge.

Project Info:
  • Platform: PC Windows10
  • Tools: Unity & C# & PhotoShop
  • Date of Completion: 2023.07
  • Controller: Mouse & Keyboard
  • Art Style: Cartoon

What are we doing

Inspiration

Today, 40 percent of the world's oceans are severely impacted by humans, and more than half of the coastal habitats are being degraded. As humans enjoy the natural gifts of the ocean, we must not forget the pain that the ocean has experienced. The team took the lack of people's understanding of the state of marine pollution as the background of the research, and created a project on the theme of the ocean, with the aim of popularizing general knowledge of the ocean and protecting the marine environment.

Our Goal

By creating a single-player game, our goal is to make people realize the importance of protecting the environment with a compelling backstory and detailed action.

The game combines a touching story with an immersive art presentation to provide players with an exquisite gaming experience.

My Duty

As the lead programmer and designer on the team, my responsibilities include crafting scene interactions and writing enemy AI to bring this game world to life.

INTRODUCTORY SCENE

The gameplay experience begins with an introductory scene that invites the player into Aqua's world with a story opening. Through on-screen transitions, the player becomes Aqua and embarks on a quest to save his friends.

Aqua, the sea spirit, is a messenger of the ocean. Sea Spirits and Whale Sharks are very good friends, they often play and swim together. At an unknown time, mankind began to endlessly dump pollutants into the ocean, and the whale sharks living in the caves mutated due to the erosion of pollutants, and kept hitting the walls of the caves in pain, and the sea was shaken by it. Hearing the whale shark's call, Aqua decides to go to the undersea cave to purify the whale shark and make it change back to its original form.

Gameplay
Main Character

Players take on the role of Aqua, a sea spirit, and use their skills to pass through various obstacles and travel to the deepest part of the ocean to purify the mutated whale sharks.

  • Aqua is the messenger of the ocean, so the level of pollution in the ocean is closely related to Aqua, who has an oxygen bar and a pollution bar, which will produce different results through different interactions with the surrounding environment:

    • In the dark, oxygen-free dead zone the oxygen bar decreases, the oxygen bar decreases to zero, and the game ends.
    • The Pollution Bar increases over time and increases dramatically when the Sea Spirit touches an oil or trash drop, is touched by an enemy, or is attacked by an enemy. Aqua will reduce the Pollution Bar by a certain amount by launching a ball of light at a mutated creature and successfully purifying the creature. Once the pollution bar is full, the game ends.
    • If Aqua is hit by a falling rock, falls into a giant vortex, or is struck by a spiked wolfsbane, the game is over!

Skills

  • Light Sphere: long press to store power, release to fire. Fire at mutated creatures to purify them; fire at dark environments to illuminate them and heal coral.
  • Compression: The Aqua shrinks in size, allowing it to pass through narrow caves.
  • Sprint: Aqua accelerates to avoid falling logs and save time.
Game Scene Design Iteration

At first, our team designed the following map, then we gradually added all kinds of organs and traps in the map, and built TileMap for user testing, then, we encountered problems:

Problem: In the initial map design, we created a vast and intricate world. However, during user testing, a player raised a critical issue - the immense open spaces made it difficult to find the path to the final boss. At the same time, players responded that they didn't know what the organs on the road were and how those organs worked on the players.

Solution: We added visual cues, landmarks, and defined pathways to guide players while revising the grand scale of the environment.

Additionally, we restructured certain areas of the map, breaking down the larger open spaces into more manageable sections connected by well-defined pathways. This not only facilitated easier navigation but also enhanced the overall flow of gameplay.

After this improvement, there was a noticeable increase in player ratings of the maps, which they felt were helpful in helping them understand the map's content and complete the flow of the game.

Coding - ENEMY SYSTEM
Customized Enemy AI System

Problem:

When creating a platform-adventure game, one of the primary concerns is designing the enemy AI system.

Initially, we employed a commercially available AI middleware for our game's enemy characters, which offered basic functionalities for patrolling, detecting the player, and engaging in combat. However, we encountered a significant challenge in achieving the desired level of complexity and intelligence in enemy behavior. Additionally, as our game heavily relies on the AI to provide dynamic and immersive gameplay experiences, we found it necessary to explore the development of a custom AI system to meet our specific requirements.

Solution:

To overcome these challenges, I studied the main ways to compose an enemy AI.

  • Conditional judgment
  • Finite state machines
  • Behaviour Tree

So, our group chose to customize the state machine to complete the writing of the enemy (specifically the Boss) AI. By calculating the state and position of the boss and the position of the player, the boss can "intelligently" track the player and make it more difficult for the player to hit the boss. In addition, our AI takes into account the collision system, so that when the player tries to get close to the boss, the boss will unleash the collision skill, so that the player will have to stay at a certain distance to fight the boss. I was responsible for the development of the entire state machine. After implementing the system and conducting several tests, we achieved remarkable results. The illustration shows the state description of the Boss.

For the Boss requirements are as follows:

  1. The enemy can automatically follow the protagonist;
  2. The enemy model has a total of four actions: Idle (Idle) Run (Run) Attack (Attack) Death (Death) Impact (Smash);
  3. Requirements of the enemy at the right time to make the right action.

First of all, we added PolygonCollider2D collider to the art material, which supports manual dragging and dropping of shapes, but it will be very troublesome if there is a need to change the collider in the game later. The state machine is then subdivided into a state machine system, a state base class, an actual script that is mounted on the object, which utilizes inheritance from the state base class to write different states, a state machine system that controls the state method calls and additions and deletions, and then the actual script on the object that registers the events to add each state in. Then use the animation frame production, in the use of animation frame events there are a few points to note:

  1. Three parameters can only pass one of the three, or no parameters.
  2. Function event function source must have an Animator component.
  3. Object if empty, then it will default to choose its own bound script as the event function provided. After we finish binding the animation frame events, we can just play the animation to automatically generate the fireballs.

So in the Boss skill, just play the animation, as well as follow the conditions and flow in the flowchart to switch the state to complete the creation of the Boss AI.
